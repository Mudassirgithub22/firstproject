All websites are somewhat interactive…we click on links or scroll a page, but truly interactive websites take us on a user-driven adventure or draws us in through motion and sound while giving us the power of choice. Interaction can be as simple as a series of clicks that navigate us through a story or landscape, or as complex as moving through a 3D environment to discover hidden content or reach goals to move to the next step. Put simply, interactivity is creating a user experience where the visitor is placed at the center. Remember the joy and wonder of stumbling across a rich web experience built in Flash? There was sound and movement and hidden ways you could interact with the scene to unravel a mystery or just have fun with the site’s interface. When Flash began to die off, there was a period of time when the joy and immersion it offered became rarer, interactive magic limited to AAA websites promoting games or movies. Today, immersive interaction can be found at the core of almost all amazing websites thanks to advances in WebGL and Javascript.
